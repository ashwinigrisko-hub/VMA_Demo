Framework Overview

An independent, data-driven way to oversee revenue-cycle outsource vendors
VMA answers three questions about every vendor from the client's own authoritative account, payment, and placement data: are they recouping payment effectively, at an acceptable cost to collect, and invoicing accurately for the accounts they actually hold? It is a backend analytics solution delivered through any front end (Concerto, standalone web app, or Ana), operating independently of source systems during daily use. Epic (or the comparable patient-accounting system) is the source of truth; every vendor claim is validated against it.

Scope boundary — VMA vs. WPA

VMA evaluates the vendor relationship: collections, cost, yield, inventory accuracy, and invoice integrity. It does not measure the individual workflow productivity of vendor staff — touch rates, time-to-first-touch, note quality, per-user output all belong to Workforce Performance Analytics (WPA). This wall is maintained throughout the product. A client who wants to monitor vendor-staff productivity buys the WPA vendor add-on and feeds vendor activity data into it.

Six Components Delivered

What the SKU does
Three reconciliation pillars plus placement strategy, alerting, and a contract database — all on a shared foundation of the client's accounts/transactions data, vendor placement and inventory files, vendor invoice data, and vendor contract terms.
Intelligence + AutomationRequired

1 · Vendor Inventory & Reconciliation

Complete view of all accounts placed across all vendors, plus automated reconciliation flagging accounts missing from vendor files, in files but unassigned, closed/paid internally but still active, duplicate placements, and recalled-but-worked. Account-level exception reporting supports operational cleanup and invoice disputes.

IntelligenceRequired

2 · Vendor Performance (Collections, Cost & Yield)

Per-vendor scorecards on recovery rate, payment yield vs. expected, cost to collect, net yield, and cash recoveries over a rolling window. Comparison is normalized for account complexity (payer mix, balance band, placement age, A/R Valuator grade). Touch-based productivity is explicitly excluded.

Intelligence + AutomationRequired

3 · Invoice Validation & Fee Validation

The largest gap and primary product driver. Each invoice line is compared against actual collections, placement/recall status, contingency terms, and contract bill rate. Payment attribution traces each payment to placement context for multi-vendor and vendor-plus-internal splits. Fee leakage analytics quantify dollars paid on accounts that likely would have paid internally.

ML + IntelligenceRequired

4 · Placement Strategy (A/R Valuator)

Surfaces A/R Valuator liquidity scores and bond-rating-style buckets at the account level, grouped by aging and balance. Performance-by-bucket shows how each vendor performs within each grade; a high-liquidity-placed-externally report flags accounts that should be retained internally. VMA informs placement; it does not automate it.

Intelligence + WorkflowRequired

5 · Exception-Based Alerting

A prebuilt alert library. Each alert carries a recommended action, owner, due date, impacted accounts, and supporting evidence; outcomes are tracked to see whether intervention improved cash or resolution.

Configuration + AlertingRequired

6 · Contract Database

A standard contract-terms repository across all vendors (fee structure, rates, effective dates, guarantees) that drives fee validation and inventory-vs-contract checks, with alerts on approaching renewal and termination-notice windows and identification of bad terms to renegotiate.

Methodology

Three reconciliation pillars
Each pillar depends on a matched-cadence data feed from both the client and the vendor, with the client A/R system as the source of truth.

Inventory Reconciliation

Monthly / bimonthly

List-match of vendor-reported inventory against client-assigned inventory — flags accounts not in the vendor report, duplicate holds, recalled-but-still-worked accounts, and balance mismatches.

Payment Attribution

Per collection cycle

Traces each payment back to placement context: was the account with the vendor at the time of payment, and does the fee claim align with contract attribution rules. Supports multi-vendor and vendor-plus-internal splits.

Invoice Validation

Per billing cycle

Audits each vendor invoice against contract terms and actual collections. This is an audit, not a list-match — which is why it is named validation rather than reconciliation.

Exception Alert Library

Prebuilt alerts — each with action, owner, and evidence

No first touch within SLA

Newly placed accounts with no first touch inside the contractual window → SLA breach notice.

Vendor recovery below expected

Recovery below normalized expectation for the assigned bucket mix → escalate to QBR.

Payment yield drop

Payment yield vs. expected falls below the 90-day baseline → investigate.

Duplicate placement

An account appears in two vendor inventory files simultaneously → resolve duplicate.

Inventory mismatch

Accounts closed/paid internally still active with a vendor → recall / reassign.

Payment without activity

Fee billed where contract requires vendor activity first, but none is logged → dispute.

Highly liquid account placed externally

AAA/AA-graded balance outsourced at contingency → retain internally.

Contract SLA breach / renewal window

Contract SLA breach, or approaching renewal / termination-notice window → review terms.
